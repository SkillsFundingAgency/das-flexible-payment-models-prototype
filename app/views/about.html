About simplified payments pilot

Our vision

A payment product that is simple for user, adaptable to different payment models, responsive to new policy and cheaper to run.

4 planned phases

  1. Mobilise a pilot (i.e. a very small number of users) – this is to prove that we can make payments using AS data (for core payments) whilst replicating current validations / controls – “creating the foundations”
  2. Utilising the support and input from wider stakeholders across DfE and ESFA to further develop payment journeys to include change of circumstances and roll-out to a private cohort of users in advance of wider roll-out
  3. Deliver any further requirements to enable the functionality to operate independently of the ILR and ensure roll-out is seamless
  4. Transition phase

To get a wider look at our work and see what we released recently, see our Roadmap.

What outcome will users get from this service?

  1. Reduced admin effort and errors for training providers and employers
  2. Reduced costs for training providers and employers
  3. Faster payments to providers
  4. Reduced lost funds
  5. Increased transparency

What outcome are we looking for?

  1. Reduced complaints to AS
  2. Reduced funds trapped in DataLock disputes
  3. Increased control over the payment process
  4. More effective support to users
  5. Reduced cost of running the service

Background

  • The payments simplification project passed the alpha stage in August 2022 and build has started
  • The solution will enable the Apprenticeship Service to calculate accurate payments based on the data the provider and employer enter for the learner when the commitment is created
  • By paying from the Apprenticeship Service, we will be aiming to greatly reduce the administrative burden for providers and employers by removing data duplication and simplifying the data lock processes
  • When change of circumstances occur, these can be raised, managed and actioned within the Apprenticeship Service – realised by simpler and more transparent user journeys, and significantly reducing the number of disruptions to user journeys, reducing the need for support, and increasing the ability to accurately forecast costs
  • Throughout the project we will be working with the wider simplification programme to take the opportunity to simplify the existing Apprenticeship funding rules
  • We will also be working with external assurance teams to ensure that our solution continues to ensure public funding is protected
